What I would track (KPIs)

For any specific devrel initiative, I try to limit the KPIs to 2-3 maximum. The goal is not to overwhelm anyone with numbers, but to ensure that we are going in the right direction. These would apply to any video content, not just this video.

Developer Hours

Time is the most precious currency anyone has. One way to see if any type of content is valuable for the target audience is to measure the number of hours the developers gave to you. In other words, I would measure the usefulness of this short video tutorial in terms of how many hours of it has been watched. I feel like it's a better KPI than the number of views cause it gives you the opportunity to track your success equally no matter the video length. Ten views of a 5-minute videos do not equal 10 views of a 30-minute one.

New (Community) Users

This would be critical to understand the impact, but it would be impossible at first since there is no CKEditor community. We could see if we have new users using CKEditor, and I assume you have observability in place to track features usage, but it's impossible to say with everything developer relations is doing, which efforts helped a specific aspect. To make it easier, we would need to create a Slack or Discord server to create and foster a healthy community.
